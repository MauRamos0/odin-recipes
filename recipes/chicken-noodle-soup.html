Chicken Noodle Soup

Description

A chicken noodle soup recipe that's a very easy, very good substitute for when you don't have time to make your soup totally from scratch. Whether you're feeling under the weather or you're just in need of a little comfort, there's nothing like homemade chicken noodle soup to warm you up from the inside out. With this shortcut recipe, you can have the best chicken noodle soup of your life on the table in just 40 minutes.

Ingredients

  • Onions and Celery: In the first step of this chicken noodle soup recipe, diced onions and chopped celery are cooked in butter until they're aromatic and tender.

  • Broth: This chicken noodle soup recipe calls for a mixture of chicken and vegetable broths. If you're short on time or ingredients, you can just use one or the other.

  • Chicken: Of course, you'll need chicken. If you have leftover rotisserie chicken, you can use that in place of the cooked chicken breasts for a budget-friendly shortcut.

  • Noodles: Make sure you don't add the noodles too early, as cooking them too long will make them mushy.

  • Carrots: Carrots, which add a pop of bright color and flavor, are added at the end of cooking so they stay crisp. You can add them to the saut√©ed vegetables at the beginning if you like your carrots tender.

  • Basil and Oregano: Dried basil and oregano lend warm, earthy flavor to this chicken noodle soup recipe.

Steps

  1. Melt butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion and celery and cook until just tender, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add chicken broth, vegetable broth, chicken, egg noodles, carrots, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes.
